This book has to be one of the most fascinating premises I've ever seen in a science-fiction book. An ambitious research physicist invents a way for a human to visit the other branching timelines that are theorized in the many worlds interpretation of quantum physics. Hoping to use this technology to correct mistakes he made in his life, he visits timelines where he made different choices. But because multiverse-travel is not time-travel, the only way to live a different life is to steal it from a parallel version of himself (and he's not going to give it up without a fight). Sounds pretty great, right? It was a gripping, fun, story, but I also appreciated how it explored the connection between our choices and our identity. How much of *who we are* is a result of our genes vs the choices that we make? Do our choices just change our trajectory, or do they also change *us*. AI recommended this book to me based on other science fiction I've read and besides being a bit more graphic than I wanted, the recommendation was right on the money.
