---

This book, written in 1956, is a history of how the American West was settled covering the years from 1840-1900. The book dives deep into the lives of influential pioneers like John C. Fremont, Brigham Young, Leland Stanford and many others, describing how each contributed to the development of the West. The book is long and detailed, covering a full history of California, Nevada, Utah, and Colorado, from their very first settlements to being granted Statehood. I learned a lot, and several stories stuck in my mind. I had heard about the Donner party, but the full story, with Lansford Hastings, his poorly planned "cutoff" route, the brutality of the Great Salt Desert, and the dissent in the party, brought a lot more context to the harrowing end. I was similarly struck by the stories of Theodore Judah and his obsession with the idea of a transcontinental railroad, Adolf Sutro's race against time to finance and build the Sutro Tunnel, and the rise and fall of Horace and "Baby Doe" Tabor. Connecting them all was this idea that the West could not be won without strong men (and women) of character. Fortunately for us, our nation delivered. ⛰️ 🇺🇸
