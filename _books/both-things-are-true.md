This book is a collection of essay written by LDS religious scholar Kate Holbrook shortly after her death. The essays cover various topics, like revelation, forgiveness, and discipleship, and while they each pay homage to the "Both Things are True" concept, the stronger connection between them all were that they were written by Kate. Each essay contains stories and experiences from Kate's life, making the book somewhat of a memoir. That's not a bad thing—when it comes to religious writings, I have a strong preference for personalized, testimonial-style messages. At the same time, I'd LOVE to explore the concept of wrestling with two seemingly contradictory ideas, and how it can be a source of spiritual and intellectual development. This book was great, but I still want to read the book I thought this was.
