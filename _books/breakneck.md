Breakneck is a book by Dan Wang that compares the US and China. Dan is a Chinese-American writer who has spent years living in each country, and is able to speak from those experiences. To Dan, the US and China are more similar than they are different, but the differences can be explained by a key idea: The U.S. has traditionally been run by lawyers, while the Chinese Communist Party tends to be run by engineers. This framework can be used to explain a lot. The lawyerly society offers strong protections for individual liberties and free speech but has also created an environment where lawsuits can freeze development and progress. The engineering society can build miles of high-speed rails but is also responsible for social engineering fiascos like the one-child policy and zero-COVID. It's a powerful idea and one that I enjoyed exploring. If you want to know more, check out [this longer, more detailed book review](https://www.noahpinion.blog/p/book-review-breakneck) by Noah Smith (which is what landed this book on my reading list in the first place).
